# Closes

The last sentence of paragraph 4, plus the sign-off. Every letter needs both.

## Closing line — pick by tone

### Standard professional
> "Thanks for considering my application."

### Warmer / inviting (preferred default)
> "I'd welcome the chance to discuss this — happy to share more on any of the above."

### When you want to flag flexibility on logistics
> "Happy to talk through any of this in more detail, in person or remote."

### When the role is senior and they expect you to bring questions
> "I have a few questions about how the role sits within your wider engineering organisation — happy to explore those in a conversation."

### Brief and confident
> "Looking forward to hearing from you."

## Sign-off — pick by formality

- **`Kind Regards,`** — neutral, safe, slight formal lean. Use when in doubt.
- **`Thanks for considering my application,`** — slightly warmer; pair with a short closing line or use as the closing line itself.
- **`Best,`** — informal, only for companies whose own voice is informal.

Always followed by your name on the next line. `Luke Blaney` for formal contexts; `Luke` for informal.

## What never to do

- Don't end with the current-focus paragraph and just stop — recurring failure mode in older letters.
- Don't sign off without a closing line — feels truncated.
- Don't pile multiple call-to-actions ("happy to chat / available for interview / open to a call") — one is enough.
