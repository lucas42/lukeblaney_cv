# Openers

Anchor sentences for paragraph 1. Pick the framing that fits the role archetype; never use the same opener twice across applications.

**Rule**: name the role in sentence 1, then make one sharp claim. Don't start with "I'm an X who…".

---

## Cyber security leadership roles (Director / Head / Principal Security Engineer)

### Direct, JD-anchored
> "I'm writing to apply for your [Role] role. I've spent [N] years leading cyber security at a high-profile media organisation operating under continuous scrutiny from nation-state actors, regulators, and a global readership."

*See [Framing decisions](#framing-decisions) for choosing N.*

### Intersection framing (when the role bridges security + engineering)
> "Your [Role] role caught my attention because it sits at the intersection of cyber security and platform engineering, two areas where I've operated as a senior leader and a hands-on technical contributor across the last decade."

### When the role wants someone who can lead through ambiguity
> "I'm interested in your [Role] role. My most recent role was setting strategic direction for cyber security at the Financial Times, including leading the function through a period of organisational restructuring and the formation of a centralised security team."

## Platform / Reliability engineering leadership (Head / Director of Platform Eng, SRE Director)

### Built-it-from-scratch
> "Your [Role] role looks like a strong fit. I built the Reliability Engineering function at the Financial Times from a blank slate: recruiting the team, defining the remit, and embedding DevOps principles across the wider engineering organisation."

### Multi-team leadership
> "I'm applying for your [Role] role. Across thirteen years at the Financial Times I've led teams responsible for observability, CDNs, developer tooling, CI/CD, single sign-on, and cyber security: every domain a platform team typically owns, and several it doesn't."

### Enablement framing (when JD emphasises developer-experience)
> "Your [Role] role is the kind of work I'm most engaged by: platform leadership where success is measured by what *other* teams can now do, not just what mine ships."

---

## Director / Head / VP of Engineering (multi-team, hybrid leadership-and-technical)

### Hybrid leadership + technical authority (when the JD says "not purely strategic")
> "I'm writing to apply for your [Role] role. The shape of it, leading multiple engineering teams while staying directly involved in architectural decisions on [domain, e.g. 'distributed, event-driven systems'], is exactly the shape of work I've spent the past several years doing at the Financial Times."

*Stands alone. Use when the JD explicitly says the Director role expects a technical authority who guides architectural decisions, not just strategy. The `[domain]` slot lets the opener anchor on the JD's specific technical signal (event-driven, distributed systems, real-time data, microservices at scale, etc.).*

### Multi-domain leadership breadth (when the JD wants someone who has run several technical functions)
> "I'm applying for your [Role] role. Across [N] years at the Financial Times, I've led teams across [list 3–4 relevant domains from this list: cyber security, reliability engineering, observability, edge delivery, publishing-platform architecture, identity/SSO, on-prem-to-SaaS migrations], and held the technical-direction responsibility alongside the leadership work rather than handing it off."

*Use when the JD wants engineering-leadership breadth without specifically wanting platform-team experience. Picking the domains matters: list only the ones relevant to the JD.*

## Architect / Principal Architect / Staff Engineer (IC-track)

### Deliberate technical re-focus (when stepping out of director-level into IC)
> "I'm interested in your [Role] role. I've spent the last several years in a director-level cyber security role at the Financial Times, but kept the Principal Engineer technical work alongside it rather than handing it off. I'm now looking for a role where that hands-on architecture is the main focus."

*Stands alone; the reader doesn't need the CV to understand the level-shift being described.*

### Breadth-of-disciplines framing
> "I have a lot of experience working on tech platforms across architecture, engineering, and cyber security, giving me an unusually rounded view of the trade-offs a modern platform has to weigh."

### Specific-platform framing (when JD names a stack or platform shape)
> "Your [Role] role looks like a natural fit. As architect for the Financial Times' Universal Publishing Platform, a platform of 100+ microservices delivering content from editorial tools to web, apps, and B2B clients, I made architectural decisions across six engineering squads while pivoting the architect role itself from decision bottleneck to consultative partner."

## Engineering Manager / Tech Lead Manager

### Leading-through-leads framing
> "I'm interested in your [Role] role. I've spent the last several years leading engineering organisations through senior tech leads: building new teams, restructuring established ones, and forming a multidisciplinary cyber security function out of pre-existing teams from different business units."

### Lifecycle framing (when JD wants someone who can join an existing team and adapt)
> "I'm applying for your [Role] role. I've managed a range of teams through different points in their lifecycles: forming new ones from scratch, taking over established ones mid-stride, and leading teams through reorganisations." *(Note: this is the boilerplate opener Luke has historically reused, only use it once, with a JD-specific second sentence.)*

## When the company is the hook

Use when the company has a public mission, distinctive culture, or domain you have a personal connection to. Lead with the company connection, then the role.

### Public-service / mission-driven
> "[Company] has always been an organisation I've found inspiring, both the high-level mission and the engineering culture behind it. I'm applying for the [Role] role in your [Team] team because…"

### Domain-passion (rail, music, transport, media…)
> "I'm a long-standing fan of [domain]: I've [specific evidence: countries, gigs, formats, communities]. So your [Role] role caught my attention immediately, a chance to bring my [N] years of [relevant experience] to a domain I genuinely care about."

## Framing decisions

### How many years of cyber security leadership?

Two honest framings, pick per JD:

- **"5 years"**: counts the Principal Engineer (Reliability Engineering, Cyber Security, Observability & Edge Delivery) work from Feb 2018 onwards. Use when the JD treats cyber security experience as a strength; Luke was doing cyber security leadership through all those years, alongside other domains.
- **"3 years"**: counts only the formally-titled cyber security leadership (Interim VP from April 2022 onwards). Use when the JD is looking for more software engineering experience and less cyber; the Principal Engineer years then count towards software engineering tenure instead.

Both are defensible. **What's not defensible** is letting the year-claims across different domains add up to more than Luke's actual working tenure. The Principal Engineer years can count for cyber *or* for software engineering in any given document, but not for both at the same time; that would imply parallel tenures that didn't exist. Before finalising a letter, total every "[N] years of [domain]" claim and sanity-check it against ~15 years of total working life.

### Other recurring claims

- **"13 years at the Financial Times"**: counts FT Labs (Dec 2011) through Cyber Security Director (March 2025). Used consistently in past letters; safe to keep.
- **"100+ microservices"**: the Universal Publishing Platform during the Architect - Content role. Accurate.
- **"6 engineering squads"**: same platform, same role. Accurate.
